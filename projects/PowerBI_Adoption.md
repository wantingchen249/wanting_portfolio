## 1. Overview

Designed and executed an enterprise-wide Power BI adoption programme for the global HR function, transforming a team of Excel-dependent HR professionals into confident, self-serve analytics users. The programme combined technical enablement, change management, governance, and community-building — achieving 200+ active users and a 90%+ monthly active usage rate within 6 months of launch.

---

## 2. Business Context & Challenge

### Organisational Situation
The HR function had recently invested in a Power BI HR Analytics platform (see `HR_Scorecard.md`). The platform was technically excellent — but adoption was lagging. Six weeks post-launch, only 23% of licensed users had logged in more than once. The investment was at risk of being classified internally as "shelfware."

Analysis revealed the adoption barrier was not the technology — it was capability, confidence, and culture.

### Core Problems
- **Low digital confidence:** Many HR Business Partners and operational HR staff had limited experience with any BI tool; Power BI felt intimidating.
- **Habit inertia:** Teams defaulted to requesting reports from the central analytics team rather than self-serving, even when dashboards were available.
- **No community:** There was no HR analytics peer community — no forum to ask questions, share tips, or celebrate wins.
- **Inconsistent skills:** Proficiency ranged from zero to advanced within the same team, making one-size-fits-all training ineffective.
- **No visibility:** HR leadership had no view of who was using what, or which reports were driving decisions.

### Stakeholder Landscape
- **Sponsors:** CHRO, VP of HR Operations
- **Target audience:** 250 HR professionals across 5 regions (HR Business Partners, HR Analysts, L&D, Recruitment, Operations)
- **Support:** IT (licensing and access), HR Analytics team (content owners)

## 3. My Role & Approach

**Role:** Programme Lead — Adoption Strategy, Training Design, Community Management

### Phase 1 — Adoption Diagnostic (Weeks 1–3)
- Analysed Power BI usage data (report opens, active users, time-in-report, bounce rates) by role and region.
- Ran a **skills survey** across 250 HR users: rated confidence with data, Excel, and Power BI on a 5-point scale.
- Conducted 20 qualitative interviews with users who had not adopted and users who had.
- Produced an **Adoption Barrier Report** identifying 5 root causes (listed above).
- Segmented users into 3 capability tiers: **Explorer** (0–1/5), **Builder** (2–3/5), **Champion** (4–5/5).

### Phase 2 — Programme Design (Weeks 4–6)
Designed a 3-track adoption programme based on capability segmentation:

| Track | Audience | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Explorer Track** | 60% of users — low confidence | Foundational data literacy + Power BI navigation |
| **Builder Track** | 30% of users — intermediate | Self-service analytics, filters, bookmarks, export |
| **Champion Track** | 10% of users — advanced | Dashboard design, DAX basics, advocacy skills |

**Programme pillars:**
1. **Learn** — Role-specific training (live + recorded + micro-learning)
2. **Practice** — Sandbox environment with safe, sample HR data
3. **Connect** — HR Analytics Community (Teams channel, monthly call)
4. **Measure** — Adoption dashboard tracking engagement weekly

### Phase 3 — Content & Sandbox Build (Weeks 5–9)
- Built a **Sandbox Power BI environment** with anonymised HR sample data — users could explore freely without fear of breaking live reports.
- Created **role-specific learning paths** on SharePoint:
  - HR Business Partner path: headcount, turnover, workforce planning views
  - Recruiter path: pipeline metrics, time-to-hire, source effectiveness
  - L&D path: training completion, compliance, learning hours
- Developed **16 micro-learning videos** (3–7 mins each): "How to use filters," "How to export to PowerPoint," "Understanding the headcount snapshot," etc.
- Designed a **Quick Reference Card** (1-page PDF per role): top 5 reports, top 5 actions, where to get help.

### Phase 4 — Training Delivery (Weeks 9–18)
- Delivered 12 live virtual training sessions (Explorer/Builder/Champion tracks) across 3 time zones.
- All sessions recorded and published to the HR Analytics Community SharePoint.
- Ran **"Office Hours"** every 2 weeks: open Q&A sessions where any HR user could bring questions about reports or data interpretation.
- Delivered 2 in-person "Data Day" workshops at regional HR conferences (APAC and EMEA).
- Embedded **Teams bot reminder** prompting users to check "Your Insight of the Week" every Monday — a curated finding from the HR dashboards, demonstrating real-world application.

### Phase 5 — Community & Sustainability (Weeks 16–24)
- Launched the **"HR Analytics Champions" programme**: 12 volunteers trained as local advocates (1 per team/region).
- Champions receive monthly briefings on new Power BI features and upcoming reports; act as first-line support within their teams.
- Established the **HR Analytics Teams channel**: 200+ members; daily questions, tips, and dashboard screenshots shared organically.
- Created a **"Data Win of the Month"** recognition: HR leaders nominate examples of data-driven decisions — winning examples shared in All-HR calls.
- Built an **Adoption Power BI Dashboard** (visible to HR leadership):
  - Monthly Active Users (MAU) by region and role
  - Report opens, session duration, most-used reports
  - Training completion rates by track
  - Community engagement (posts, responses)
  - Self-serve vs. analyst-requested report ratio

### Phase 6 — Measurement & Iteration (Ongoing)
- Weekly adoption metrics review with HR Operations lead.
- Monthly community health check: engagement rate, unanswered questions, topic trends.
- Quarterly training refresh: new reports, new features, updated HR scenarios.
- Annual skills re-survey to track capability progression.

## 7. Lessons Learned

1. **Technology is not the barrier — confidence is.** The reports were good; the blocker was users not believing they could use them. The adoption programme proved that investment in people capability delivers more ROI than investment in another tool.
2. **Segmentation is everything.** One training for 250 people of varying skill levels would have served no one. Three targeted tracks — with different vocabulary, pace, and examples — made every participant feel the training was built for them.
3. **Champions amplify reach exponentially.** 12 trained advocates — embedded in teams, approachable by peers — generated more sustained adoption than 12 formal training sessions. Peer influence is the most powerful change mechanism.
4. **Measure the programme, not just the output.** Building the Adoption Dashboard meant we could see adoption curves in real time and intervene (targeted outreach, extra office hours) when specific regions lagged. Without measurement, we'd have declared success too early.
5. **Recognition drives culture.** "Data Win of the Month" was low-cost and high-impact. Seeing a peer recognised for using a dashboard to justify a hiring decision made every HR professional want to be next.
